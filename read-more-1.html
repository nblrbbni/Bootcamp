The difference between Robusta and Arabica Coffee

Robusta and Arabica coffee beans have been used in Indonesia for decades. Both types of coffee are very popular in the world. In fact, 75% of the world coffee market is controlled by arabica, the rest is robusta.

But in Indonesia, robusta coffee is more common. Even today, Indonesia is still the fourth largest robusta coffee producer in the world, after Brazil, Vietnam and Colombia.

And here are 5 differences between robusta and arabica coffee that you need to know :


1. Differences in Seed Shape

2. Planting Location.

3. Fragrance and Flavor Produced

4. Caffeine Content

5. Price

About

Coffee is a company engaged in coffee beverages that enlivens the contemporary coffee market in Indonesia. Coffee is considered successful in filling the price gap between expensive international retail coffee and packaged instant coffee served at coffee shops. Coffee's brand prestige is getting stronger along with the development of Coffee's business, especially since it received funding injections from several venture capitals, such as Sequoia India, Arrive, Serena Ventures and Alpha JWC Ventures. Coffee plans to expand its market overseas by introducing the distinctive taste of Indonesian coffee.
